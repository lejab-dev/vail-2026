Eagle Bahn Gondola + Epic DiscoveryActivityMust Do
🚶 ~2 min walk from Landmark
📅 Jun 13–Sept 1, 2025 · Daily 9:30am–4pm
⏰ 3–4 hours

Ride the Eagle Bahn Gondola (#19) from right next to the Landmark — vertical rise 2,125 ft, 8 min ride. Epic Discovery at Eagle's Nest: Forest Flyer Mountain Coaster (thrilling for ages 6–9), Marmot Mini Kids Tubing (gentle, great for little ones), Little Eagle Kids Zipline (10 ft high — confidence-builder), Bungee Trampoline, 35-ft Climbing Wall, and Nature Discovery Center (giant yurt with animal displays, puzzles, trivia — great for all ages). 🌐 Epic Discovery Info
Pro tip: Jacket required — it's 10–15°F cooler at the summit. Closed-toe shoes mandatory for all activities. Buy Adventure Pass online to save time. June 22 is only 2 days after the summer solstice — incredible late-evening golden light if you go up near closing.

🥾 Big Hike

Morning — Start Early
🥾
Booth Falls TrailHikeMust Do
📏 4.7 mi RT (from shuttle stop)
⬆️ 1,440 ft gain
⏰ 2.5–3 hrs (to falls & back)
🚌 ~15 min from Landmark via East Vail bus
🏔 Eagles Nest Wilderness

Most popular hike in the Vail area and for good reason. Steady climb through spectacular aspen groves and sandstone formations along Booth Creek to a 60-ft waterfall. Starting elevation 9,397 ft. The waterfall at mile 2 is the perfect turnaround for families with young kids — great rocks for scrambling and splashing. Well-marked trail. Older kids (6–9) will be captivated by the creek and boulders along the way.

Trailhead access (no car parking as of 2025): Park at Gore Creek or Red Sandstone parking garage, then take the free East Vail bus from Vail Transportation Center (free, frequent service). Restrooms at trailhead. Target 7–8am start to beat crowds.

No trail fee · Free bus · Eagles Nest Wilderness overnight permits required for camping (day hike is free)
🥾 AllTrails 📖 Full Guide
Pro tip: Carry the 1-year-old in a backpack carrier. Bring a picnic for the waterfall rocks — great spot to eat lunch before heading back. Start at over 9,400 ft — take first 20 min slowly and drink lots of water. Late June means you're close to peak wildflower timing for this area.

Piney River Ranch — Lake + HikingActivityMust Do
🚗 12 mi north of Landmark (~25 min drive)
📅 Opens June 21 · Daily 9am–6pm
💰 SUP/Canoe: $40/hr · First-come basis

Vail's most breathtaking day-trip destination — a private 40-acre retreat at the edge of Piney Lake set against the jagged Gore Range. Canoes and SUPs available first-come ($40/hr, paddles and life vests included — PFDs mandatory for kids under 12). Glassy, calm water perfect for all skill levels including beginners and kids. After paddling, hike the Upper Piney River / Piney Falls Trail (6 mi RT) directly from the Ranch through wildflower meadows, aspen groves, and along the river with towering Mt. Powell in the distance. Abundant wildlife: moose, bear, deer, eagles, marmots, foxes.

No entry fee to Ranch · SUP/Canoe ~$40/hr · Fishing requires CO fishing license (cpw.state.co.us). No equipment rentals for fishing — bring your own.
🌐 Piney River Ranch 🥾 Upper Piney Trail (AllTrails)
Pro tip: Arrive early (9–10am) to beat crowds for canoe/SUP rentals. Bring a full packed lunch for the fire pit area — dining at the Ranch has limited options. June 25 falls just after opening week — less crowded than peak July.

Shrine Ridge TrailHikeMust Do
📏 4.6 mi RT
⬆️ 1,043 ft gain
⏰ 2–2.5 hrs
🚗 ~18 mi from Landmark (~25 min drive)
🅿️ Free parking at Shrine Pass TH

Located between Vail Pass and Copper Mountain — take I-70 Exit 190 (Vail Pass Summit), follow signs for Shrine Pass Road (~2.3 mi on good dirt road, SUV preferred). Trail begins at obvious parking area with vault toilet. Winds through open pine meadows past two small ponds and up to spectacular Shrine Ridge at 11,745 ft — panoramic views of the Gore Range, Sawatch Range, and a direct sightline to Mount of the Holy Cross. Late June is right at the start of wildflower season here — columbines, paintbrush, and lupine will be emerging.

No fee · No permit required · Free parking (arrive by 8am — lot fills). No cell service at trailhead — download AllTrails map offline first.
🥾 AllTrails 📖 Wildflower Guide
Pro tip: Afternoon thunderstorms are common by noon–1pm at this elevation — plan to be off the exposed ridge by then. June 26 morning should give excellent clear conditions. Kids ages 6–9 will love the open meadows for running around. Easy to turn back early if the 1-year-old gets tired.

Morning

Drive to BreckenridgeTransit

Landmark → Breckenridge: ~45 min (I-70 E to Exit 203, south on Hwy 9). Breckenridge is a charming historic mining town at 9,600 ft elevation — Colorado's best-preserved Victorian mountain town. Leave by 8–9am to maximize the day.

🚡
BreckConnect GondolaGondolaFree!
📍 170 Watson Ave, Breckenridge
📅 Mid-June–Labor Day · Daily 9:30am–5:30pm
💰 100% FREE for all riders
⏰ 13 min ride · 7,592 ft long

One of the best free mountain activities in Colorado — the BreckConnect Gondola connects downtown Breckenridge to the base of Peak 8 with stunning views of Cucumber Gulch and the Tenmile Range. Stops: Breckenridge Station → Shock Hill → Peak 7 → Peak 8 Base Camp. No ticket, no reservation — just walk on. 121 gondola cars, up to 8 passengers each. At Peak 8: hiking, on-mountain dining at Ski Hill Grill, scenic chairlift rides ($), and the Gold Runner Alpine Coaster.

Gondola itself FREE · On-mountain activities (alpine coaster, ropes course) have separate fees — purchase at breckenridge.com or ticket office. Summit Stage free public bus connects from Frisco/Dillon area.
🌐 Breckenridge.com

Rainbow Lake via Peaks Trail (Frisco / Breck)Hike
📏 1.6 mi RT (easy) or 4.8 mi loop (moderate)
⬆️ 157 ft (easy) or 620 ft (loop)
⏰ 40 min–2.5 hrs depending on route
🚗 ~10 min from Breck gondola
🅿️ Free parking at 2nd Ave & S Cabin Green, Frisco

A beautiful and accessible mountain lake hike right between Breckenridge and Frisco in White River National Forest. The easy out-and-back (1.6 mi) leads directly to Rainbow Lake along the Peaks Trail — rolling terrain through lodgepole pine and aspen forest, lovely and peaceful. For older kids, extend to the full 4.8-mi loop via Miners Creek with mountain views and creek crossings. A Summit Stage free shuttle is available from Breckenridge to the trailhead area.

No fee · No permit required · Free parking. Limited parking — arrive early on weekends.
🥾 AllTrails (Easy Route) 🥾 AllTrails (Full Loop)
Pro tip: Combine gondola + Rainbow Lake into one efficient morning — ride gondola up, enjoy Peak 8 area for 30 min, then drive to Frisco for the hike. Back to Vail by early afternoon.

Vail Essentials

🌅 Sun & Weather — June 22–29, 2026

  • Sunrise: ~5:38–5:42 AM · Sunset: ~8:28–8:30 PM · ~14 hrs 50 min of daylight
  • Late June weather in Vail: warm days (70s°F in town, 50s°F on mountain), cool nights (40–50s°F)
  • Afternoon thunderstorms are common daily in June–August — plan ridge hikes to finish by noon–1pm
  • Wildflower season: early bloom by June 22. Peak wildflowers shift to mid-July at higher elevations

🚌 Getting Around Vail

  • Free in-town bus (Town of Vail): Connects Lionshead, Vail Village, East Vail, West Vail. Frequent service. Use for Booth Falls (East Vail bus) and most in-town trips.
  • Car (Budget Minivan): Needed for Piney River Ranch, Shrine Ridge (I-70 Exit 190), Breckenridge, Beaver Creek, Nottingham Lake. Free parking at Landmark.
  • Walking: Lionshead → Vail Village ~10 min. Betty Ford Gardens ~12 min. Little Diner + Sunbird Park ~2–3 min.
  • Free shuttle to Vail Village: Available from Lionshead — check schedule at front desk.

⛰ Altitude & Safety

  • Vail: 8,150 ft · Hike trailheads: 9,400–11,000 ft · Ridge summits: 11,000–11,700 ft
  • Day 1–2: Acclimate — stay hydrated, avoid overexertion, rest, short walks only
  • Drink 2–3x normal water on hike days · Kids dehydrate faster at altitude
  • Altitude symptoms (headache, nausea, fatigue): slow down, descend, hydrate
  • Sunscreen is critical — UV intensity is much higher at elevation. Kids especially
  • Lightning: off all exposed ridges by noon–1pm in summer
  • Wildlife: stay 75 ft from moose and bear. Carry bear spray on remote hikes (Piney)

👶 Kid-Specific Tips (Ages 1–9)

  • Age 1: Hiking carrier/backpack required. Gondola, Betty Ford Gardens, Sunbird Park, Gore Creek walks, Piney Lake shoreline. All great.
  • Ages 3–5: Pirateship Park, Piney River Ranch (shore + first mile of trail), Epic Discovery kids activities, all playgrounds.
  • Ages 6–9: All hikes work — Booth Falls (waterfall turnaround), Shrine Ridge open meadows, Rainbow Lake easy loop, Eagle's Loop.
  • Pack: layers, rain jacket, snacks, sunscreen, carrier, refillable water bottles, AllTrails downloaded offline.
  • Carrier or stroller: Mountain terrain — a quality structured carrier (Osprey, Deuter) is better than a stroller on trails.

🛒 Groceries & Supplies

  • City Market, Vail: Full grocery store ~1 mile from Landmark. Stock up on arrival for 7 nights.
  • Landmark Convenience Store: On-site for quick items, snacks, drinks.
  • Stock: sunscreen (SPF 50+), trail snacks, packed lunch supplies (cooler), water bottles, bug spray, first aid kit.
  • Colorado fishing license: cpw.state.co.us if fishing Piney Lake.
