🏔 Eagles Nest Wilderness

Most popular hike in the Vail area and for good reason. Steady climb through spectacular aspen groves and sandstone formations along Booth Creek to a 60-ft waterfall. Starting elevation 9,397 ft. The waterfall at mile 2 is the perfect turnaround for families with young kids — great rocks for scrambling and splashing. Well-marked trail. Trailhead access (no car parking as of 2025): Park at Gore Creek or Red Sandstone parking garage, then take the free East Vail bus from Vail Transportation Center (free, frequent service). Restrooms at trailhead. Target 7–8am start to beat crowds.

No trail fee · Free bus · Eagles Nest Wilderness overnight permits required for camping (day hike is free)

⛰ Altitude & Safety

  • Vail: 8,150 ft · Hike trailheads: 9,400–11,000 ft · Ridge summits: 11,000–11,700 ft
  • Day 1–2: Acclimate — stay hydrated, avoid overexertion, rest, short walks only
  • Drink 2–3x normal water on hike days · Kids dehydrate faster at altitude
  • Altitude symptoms (headache, nausea, fatigue): slow down, descend, hydrate
  • Sunscreen is critical — UV intensity is much higher at elevation. Kids especially
  • Lightning: off all exposed ridges by noon–1pm in summer
  • Wildlife: stay 75 ft from moose and bear. Carry bear spray on remote hikes

👶 Kid-Specific Tips (Ages 1–9)

  • Age 1: Hiking carrier/backpack required. Gondola, Betty Ford Gardens, Sunbird Park, Gore Creek walks, playgrounds all great.
  • Ages 3–5: Pirateship Park, Epic Discovery kids activities, first 1–2 miles of Berrypicker, all playgrounds.
  • Ages 6–9: All hikes work — Booth Falls (waterfall turnaround), Shrine Ridge open meadows, Rainbow Lake easy loop, Eagle's Loop.
  • Pack: layers, rain jacket, snacks, sunscreen, carrier, refillable water bottles, AllTrails downloaded offline.
  • Mountain terrain — a quality structured carrier (Osprey, Deuter) is better than a stroller on trails.
